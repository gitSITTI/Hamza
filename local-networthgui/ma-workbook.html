DSCR Optimizer

Massage the deal until the bank says yes. Each lever recalculates coverage and max supportable price.

How Banks Calculate DSCR
DSCR = EBITDA ÷ Annual Senior Debt Service
Banks use senior-only debt in most SBA deals — subordinated seller notes are excluded. DSCR ≥ 1.25 is the minimum for most lenders (1.20 with compensating factors).

4 Levers to Hit 1.25×: Extend amortization → lower payment → higher DSCR. Add IO period → zero principal years → higher DSCR. More seller note → smaller bank loan → higher DSCR. Negotiate price down → smaller everything → higher DSCR.

Creative Financing Structures

Six structures beyond standard SBA. Each one is a tool to close deals that don't fit the box.

Dan Peña — QLA Framework

Quantum Leap Advantage methodology. Not theory — a battle-tested playbook for acquiring businesses with other people's money.

"Your biggest challenge isn't someone else. It's the bullshit story you keep telling yourself as to why you can't achieve your dream."
— Dan Peña, Guthrie Castle seminars
🎯 Target Selection (HPT)
High Performance Targets (HPTs):
✓ Recurring, predictable cash flow
✓ Owner-operated, aging owner (succession gap)
✓ Priced below market (family biz, no broker)
✓ Essential service (recession-resistant)
✓ Below-radar for PE firms (<$5M EBITDA)
✓ Multiple below 4x EBITDA

Avoid:
✗ Trendy / fad industries
✗ Single-customer concentration >30%
✗ Owner IS the business (no transferability)
✗ Overpriced (seller greed = negotiation failure)
📞 Initial Approach
Peña's Direct Approach — No Brokers:
1. Handwritten letter to owner (not email)
2. Call within 72 hours of letter
3. Open: "I want to buy your business."
4. Never show enthusiasm or urgency
5. Let them talk — gather intel
6. First meeting: listen 80%, talk 20%

Early Intel to Gather:
• Why are they selling? (succession, health, divorce?)
• Any debt, liens, outstanding litigation?
• Is owner willing to finance part of the deal?
• What's their timeline?
"Never tell the seller how much you want his business. You can always negotiate down. Never up."
📄 The LOI (Letter of Intent)
Get LOI signed FAST. Key terms:
• Purchase price (start below fair value)
• Exclusivity period: 60–90 days (no-shop)
• Good faith deposit: $5k–$25k refundable
• Seller non-compete: 3–5yr, 25+ mile radius
• Key employee retention clause
• Seller representation + warranties
• Subject to financing and DD

Earnout clause example:
"Additional $X payable if revenue exceeds $Y in Year 1–2 post-close" — bridges valuation gap without overpaying upfront.
💰 OPM Capital Stack (QLA Style)
Other People's Money — The Peña Stack:
■ SBA / Bank: 55–70% · low rate · longest term
■ Seller Note: 20–35% · IO, subordinated
■ Mezzanine: 0–10% · PIK if possible
■ Equity: <10% ideally · from investors, not you

The zero-equity deal:
Business has working capital → fund equity from target's own cash. SBA allows this as "seller discretionary working capital injection."
"Use OPM — other people's money. OPC — other people's credibility. OPE — other people's experience."
🔍 Due Diligence — Peña's Non-Negotiables
Verify EVERYTHING. Bank statements are truth.
1. 3 years bank statements → reconcile to P&L
2. Every SDE add-back → documented receipt
3. AR aging → who owes money and how old
4. Top 10 customers → interview 3–5 yourself
5. Licenses → ARE THEY TRANSFERABLE?
6. Environmental → especially real estate
7. QoE report → >$750k deal, non-negotiable

Kill deal immediately if:
✗ Bank statements don't match P&L
✗ Key licenses not transferable
✗ Customer concentration >40% in one client
✗ Litigation you didn't know about
🏆 100-Day Post-Acquisition Plan
First 100 days — DO NOT blow up the machine:
Days 1–30: Observe. Meet everyone. Don't change anything.
Days 31–60: Install reporting (weekly P&L, cash flow)
Days 61–90: Attack biggest expense line. Renegotiate supplier contracts. Find top revenue lever.
Days 91–100: Set 12-month targets. Identify acquisition #2.

Peña's rules:
• Keep all key employees for 90 days minimum
• Don't raise prices until you understand the biz
• Cash flow statement weekly, not monthly
• Use business cash flow to fund your next deal
"Your first deal is your best deal. Don't wait for perfect. Done beats perfect every time."

2. Bitcoin has a fixed 21M supply. Demand grows with global adoption. Supply cannot respond to price. This is the opposite of every fiat currency, every central bank, and most assets.
3. Bitcoin's SF ratio (post-4th halving: ~120) exceeds gold (~60). Historically, higher SF = higher price in S2F model. Gold took 5,000 years to accumulate its stock. BTC is harder.
4. Institutional adoption is early. Strategy bought at $65K avg. MicroStrategy's thesis: BTC captures value from deteriorating monetary systems.
5. If BTC captures even 10% of gold's market cap ($13T), that implies ~$620,000/BTC. 50% of gold = ~$3.1M/BTC.
