The Wheel Strategy

Sell puts → get assigned → sell calls → repeat. Generate income on stocks you want to own anyway.

💵
1. Sell CSP
Sell put at strike below market. Collect premium. Keep cash as collateral.
→
📉
2. If Assigned
Buy 100 shares at strike. Cost basis = strike − premium collected.
→
📞
3. Sell CC
Sell covered call above your cost basis. Collect more premium.
→
🔄
4. If Called Away
Shares sold at call strike. Take profit. Return to Step 1.
→
🔁
5. Repeat
Keep rolling. Premium compounds. Goal: annualized 20-35% on capital.

Margin Manager

Know your leverage, margin cost, and safe operating zone at all times.

Margin Cost Calculator
Margin Safety Rules
SAFE Margin utilization <50% — plenty of buffer for drawdowns
CAUTION 50–75% utilization — one bad day could trigger a call
DANGER >75% utilization — margin call risk in a 15-20% drawdown

Margin call math: If portfolio drops 20% and you're at 60% utilization, your effective leverage jumps to ~75%+ fast. Always leave ≥40% buffer.

Margin vs. Income ETF yield spread: Need ≥3% spread to justify the risk. Example: JEPQ 10% yield − Robinhood 5.75% = 4.25% net (reasonable). QYLD 12% − Schwab 8.5% = 3.5% net (thin — one distribution cut wipes it).

Strategy Guide

Options income strategies — when to use each, what you're risking, what you're earning.

Cash-Secured Put (CSP)

What it is: Sell a put option below the market. You agree to buy 100 shares at the strike price if assigned. Collect the premium immediately.
Best for: Stocks you want to own at a lower price anyway. High IV environments. Cash sitting in account.
Risk: Stock crashes below your strike — you own shares at above-market price. Mitigate with quality underlyings (SPY, QQQ, JEPI).
Target return: 1–3% per month on capital secured. Equals 12–36% annualized.
Best accounts: Margin account (naked puts) or cash account (CSP). NOT Roth unless cash-secured.

Covered Call (CC)

What it is: Own 100 shares, sell a call above market. If stock reaches strike, shares get called away. You keep the premium regardless.
Best for: Stocks you own that aren't moving much. Generating income on flat/slow-growth holdings.
Risk: Caps your upside if stock rockets past strike. You miss the gain above your strike.
Target return: 0.5–2% per month on stock value. Best on high-IV stocks.
Pro tip: Sell CCs on income ETFs (JEPI, QYLD) — you get their distribution AND option premium.

Iron Condor

What it is: Sell an OTM call spread + OTM put spread. Profit if stock stays in a range. Max profit = net credit, max loss = spread width − credit.
Best for: Low-volatility, range-bound markets. SPX/SPY/QQQ in calm periods.
Risk: Defined max loss. Lose if market moves sharply either direction.
Target return: 3–8% on margin in 30–45 days.
TOS advantage: ThinkOrSwim has the best condor visualization tools. Use the Analyze tab.

Bull Put Spread (BPS)

What it is: Sell a higher put, buy a lower put. Net credit. Profit if stock stays above your short strike. Defined risk.
Best for: Bullish to neutral outlook. Lower capital requirement than naked puts. Works in Roth IRAs.
Risk: Max loss = spread width − credit. E.g., $5 spread for $1.50 credit = max loss $3.50.
Target return: 20–50% return on risk per trade.
Robinhood approved: Yes, Level 2 options. Great starter strategy.

0DTE / Weekly Strategy

What it is: Selling 0-day (expires today) or weekly options on SPX/SPY. Fastest theta decay = highest relative premium for days held.
Best for: Active traders who monitor positions. Can do CSPs or spreads 0-2 DTE.
Risk: No time for recovery if wrong. Gap risk on open. Requires discipline.
Target return: $50–$500/day on a $50k account at 10–40 delta. Weekly compounding is powerful.
Best platform: ThinkOrSwim (TOS) for 0DTE — Level 2 quotes, fast execution, SPX weekly chains.

Poor Man's Covered Call (PMCC)

What it is: Buy a long-dated deep ITM call (LEAPS) as a cheaper stock substitute. Sell short-dated OTM calls against it. Same income as CC, less capital.
Best for: High-priced stocks you can't afford 100 shares of. SPY at $550 needs $55k — LEAPS might cost $8k.
Risk: LEAPS value erodes if stock drops. Not fully delta-neutral.
Target return: 5–15% per month on LEAPS cost (versus 1-2% on full stock value for CCs).
TOS approved: Level 3 options required. Great for high-ticket growth stocks.
